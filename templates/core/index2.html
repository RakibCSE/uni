Game Design

In this multi-lesson project pupils design and code a computer game. It uses a rainforest theme but can be adapted to any topic, with learners of varying age or ability.

{#
#} {#
The project includes:
#} {#
#}
Decomposition of the elements of a game
Designing the game
Creating assets for the game, such as background images and characters
Coding the game using Scratch
Debugging and improving the code

The pupils finally present their game to classmates, explaining how they have used efficient coding techniques such as repetition, use of variables and selection.

Film & Media

This unit gives pupils the opportunity to learn how to create short videos in groups. As they progress through this unit, they will be exposed to topic-based language and develop the skills of capturing, editing, and manipulating video. Active learning is encouraged through guided questions and by working in small groups to investigate the use of devices and software.

{#
#} {#
The project includes:
#} {#
#}
What is video?
Filming techniques
Using a storyboard
Planning a video
Importing and editing video
Video evaluation

Pupils are guided with step-by-step support to take their idea from conception to completion. At the teacher’s discretion, the use of green screen can be incorporated into this unit. At the conclusion of the unit, learners have the opportunity to reflect on and assess their progress in creating a video.

Social Media

This unit gives pupils the opportunity to work on a mini group project. This project allows pupils to put into practice everything they have been taught so far. Pupils will be asked to come up with a new product or service. For example, students may develop a new game, a new movie, pair of trainers or new headphones. After having created the product, they will then go onto plan and develop a marketing strategy. This may involve creating a website, logo, content (video) and the use of social media websites such as facebook, Instagram and tiktok.
